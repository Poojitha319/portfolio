# The Day I Almost Lost Myself — And Fought Back

*From the girl who stumbled, reflected, and rose again.*

Everyone talks about success during placements — the offers, the prep, the final day smiles. But very few talk about the side that's hidden: the pressure, the burnout, the quiet guilt that creeps in.

This isn't a post I planned to write. It's one I needed to write — for myself, and maybe for someone out there who's also feeling lost.

## The Breaking Point

There was a moment in my life when the pressure broke me.

The weight of placements... the race... the fear of being left behind... It became too much. I made a mistake — one that didn't align with the person I truly am. For the first time, I saw myself do something I never believed I was capable of: **I compromised on my values.**

And in that moment, guilt flooded in. Not because I got caught. But because I knew — *this isn't me.* I wasn't afraid of people judging me. I was afraid I might stop recognizing myself.

## The Doubt

I had always been the girl who built with love, who learned with fire, who took pride in her honesty. But in that brief dark hour, I doubted everything:

*"Did I really earn anything I achieved?"*
*"Is all my hard work tainted now?"*

But here's what I realized: **One mistake doesn't undo a lifetime of sincerity.** What defines me is not the fall — but how I rise from it.

And I did rise.

## The Promise to Myself

That night, I didn't just apologize to myself... I promised myself:

- I will never cheat again — not in code, not in life
- I will honor my journey, my sweat, my dreams
- I will be proud of the girl who stood back up and said, *"I am still Poojitha. And I'm not done yet."*

## A Note to My Future Self

To the future me reading this — if ever the pressure starts building again... if you feel tempted to take the easy way out... come back here. Read these words.

You've already made that mistake once. You don't need to go through that pain again. You are strong enough to walk your path with honesty and grace.

You don't need shortcuts — because you are the long road kind of girl, the kind who turns every step into a story worth telling.

*With love and strength,*
*— The Real You, who chose to be better.*

## To Whoever Needs This

If you're under pressure, if you're tempted to take shortcuts, if you're doubting yourself — pause. Breathe. You are more than this moment.

And if you've made a mistake... don't hide from it. Learn from it. Use it to rise.

I did. And I'm still here. Still building. Still becoming.

**I'm back — stronger, clearer, and more myself than ever.**

*Until then, happy coding :)*
