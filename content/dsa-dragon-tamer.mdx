# How I Accidentally Became a Dragon Tamer

DSA isn't just technical material — it's a series of challenges that test patience, logic, and courage. Each algorithm is a dragon waiting to be tamed.

## The Sorting Dragon

This one brings chaos. Pure, unfiltered disorder.

At first, I tried brute force — like trying to organize an entire library by randomly picking up books. Then I discovered **Merge Sort** and **Quick Sort** — strategies, not just solutions. It was like learning that organizing your exam notes by topic *before* studying makes everything ten times easier.

**Lesson:** Order before action. Always.

## The Recursion Dragon

Cunning and tricky, this dragon leads you into endless loops if you're not careful.

Recursion requires something counterintuitive: **trust**. Trust that the function will call itself correctly. Trust that the base case will catch you. It's like dividing a massive assignment into daily tasks and trusting that each day's work adds up.

**Lesson:** Break the big into small. Trust the process.

## The Labyrinth Dragon (Graphs)

Vast and overwhelming — like navigating an unmapped city with no GPS.

**DFS** became my flashlight in dark corridors. **BFS** became my systematic street-by-street explorer. Together, they turned the impossible maze into a solvable puzzle.

**Lesson:** Explore strategically. Not every path needs to be walked.

## The Wise Dragon (Dynamic Programming)

Ancient and immovable, this dragon doesn't fight — it teaches.

DP taught me that **memoization** and **tabulation** are just fancy words for "remember what you've already solved." Like keeping summary notes for exam revision instead of re-reading entire textbooks.

**Lesson:** Memory is power. Don't solve the same problem twice.

## More Dragons Await

There are more dragons out there — Greedy algorithms, Backtracking, Segment Trees. I haven't tamed them all yet.

But here's the thing: I'm no longer afraid. Each dragon I've faced gave me not just a technique, but a **mindset**. And that's worth more than any algorithm.

One problem at a time. One dragon at a time.

*Until then, happy coding :)*
